The 123 BAKERY PROJECT

OVERVIEW

  • The "123 Bakery" project is a national-funded math intervention project with a cross-functional team.
  • I led the team to create a PC game called 123 BAKERY, and conduct intervention studies.
  • This webpage focuses on the UX research part of the project including two usability testing case examples.

    Lead Researcher & Project Manager

    Duration

      Researh Scheme

      Backgrounds

    • "123 Bakery" was designed for training approximate number system (ANS).
    • The ANS refers to manipulation of nonsymbolic numerical quantities without counting.
    • The main purpose of the project was to test whether trained ANS abilities were transferred to formal math abilities.

      • Researching existing programs

    • Some existing programs only focus on a single part of numerical ability (i.e., symbolic number-line estimation).
    • Other existing programs included symbolic (i.e., Arabic) calculation, which is not matched to primary research purposes.
    • Graphics and interfaces of those programs are less engaging.

          Case 1. Feedback Functions of the Estimation game

          Context

        • The estimation game asks players to place the number of nuts on the horizontal number line.
        • Because the nuts were presented very briefly, users had to estimate the range of the number.
        • The correct answer was presented as a range (+-10% of the correct answer).

          •  

           

          Challenges

        • Participants responded that the feedback is too rough and challenging to understand.
        • Responses from interviews:
          • "This game is too difficult."
          "Why this yellow line is the correct response?"
          "I want to check my response."

           

          Analysis

          Hypothesis

        • If the correct answer is given, players will better compare the difference between their response and the correct answer.
        • Players will be more motivated if there's more specific and dynamic feedback.

          •  

          Solutions

        • Adding a variety of feedback
        • Players will be more motivated if there's more specific and dynamic feedback.

            •  

             

            Results

          • Enjoyment and functionality were increased. The validity of the training was also increased.
          • Received positive feedbacks:
            • “The game was fun. I want a big clap.”
            “Estimation game is my favorite. It becomes very fun when I was playing hard levels.”
          • In the after survey, children responded that the estimation game was the most enjoyable, and the playing scores also showed that most children are very engaged in this game.

            Case 2. “Putting Candles on the Cake” Game

            Context

          • This game asks players to put candles on the cake matched to the age of the animal character.
          • The game initially asked players to ‘drag and drop’ each candle on the cake.

            •  

            Challenges

          • Children spent too much time dragging each candle on the cake.
          • Playing data showed that children did an average of 4.2 trials during the total playing time of the game, which is 5-10 times smaller than the other games.
          • This is not desirable, because the amount of training should be similar between sub-games.

            •  

             

             

            Analysis

            Hypothesis

          • The dragging time was long because the candles are too far from the top of the cake.
          • The error rate would've been increased by dragging mistakes.
          • Because there is no time limit of a trial, children would spend too much time for one trial when they were stuck, losing interests, etc.

            •  

            Solutions

          • The location of the candle box becomes closer to the cake.
          • The time limit for a trial was added as 10 secs.

            •  

             

            Results & Remained Challeges

          • The average number of completed trials was increased to 7.2 trials.
          • However, this number is still significantly smaller than other games.
          • Another issue was detected that some children spent too much time putting on candles in a ‘prettier’ way (e.g., making the alignment of candles symmetrical).

            •  

            Analysis

            Hypothesis

          • Compared to ‘drag & drop’, ‘click & add’ would decrease the time for completing a trial.
          • If there is an ‘Undo’ button that cancels the last candle a player puts on, players will be able to correct their mistakes immediately. Thus, the error rate will be expected to be decreased.

            •  

            Solutions

          • The ‘drag & drop’ way changed to the ‘click & add’ way. Once the player clicks a candle, it is placed randomly on the cake.
          • An ‘Undo’ button was added.

            •  

             

            Results

          • The average completed trials were significantly increased to 21.7 trials. This makes players go to the next level faster with enough practice.
          • The error rate is decreased.
