Development and Validation of Math Anxiety Questionaire for Korean College Students¶

Objective:¶

  • This project was conduted to develop and validate a math anxiety rating scale for korean college students through exploratory factor analysis, a reliability analysis, and confirmatry factor analysis.
  • Link of the paper published: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/318311512_Development_and_validation_of_the_Korean_mathematics_anxiety_rating_scale_for_college_students

Context:¶

  • Previous MA studies conducted in South Korea only dealt with subdomains of MA measured from elementary to high school students. However, considering that the context in which college students experience MA differs from younger students, it is important to include measurements of MA in relation to decisions about jobs, higher degrees or personal finance.
  • To develop this scale, we included previously proposed factors of MA along with additional factors tailored to college students.

Dataset:¶

  • 229 Korean college students were administered an initial survey with 55 questions with 5 factors.

Four factors of Math Anxiety:¶

  • Abstraction and language: Anxiety caused by the abstract and elusive nature of mathematics and math terminology
  • Lack of confidence in math: Lack of confidence or self-efficacy in mathematics
  • Math test anxiety : Anxiety or worry caused by being evaluated on math skills in daily life or in learning situations
  • Math learning anxiety: Anxiety in learning situations

Math Anxiety Scale (Full Questions):¶

Abstraction and language¶

  • Math makes me nervous because its terminology is more abstract.
  • I get more nervous when I see symbols such as x and y instead of numbers like 2 or 3.
  • Having to prove a mathematical theorem (e. g. triangle theorem, central limit theorem, etc) makes me nervous.
  • I feel nervous when I see complicated mathematical symbols such as ∞,$$\lim_{x \rightarrow x_0 } f(x) = l$$
  • I get nervous when I hear mathematical terms whose meaning cannot be inferred.

Lack of confidence in math¶

  • I was nervous when checking my math exam results.
  • I was nervous and could not concentrate during math exams as I was approaching the time limit.
  • I was worried that I would make calculation mistakes during math exams.
  • Taking math tests were harder for me cause it required more concentration and attention to detail.

Math test anxiety¶

  • I felt nervous in math class.
  • I felt nervous and did not enjoy math class because I could not catch up.
  • I did not like math class because I did not like my teacher.
  • When I was studying math, I was worried about my competence.
  • I was nervous and anxious during math exams.

Math learning anxiety¶

  • I easily lose confidence in math when encountered with something that seems even just a little bit difficult.
  • I think I have no potential for math.
  • I cannot study math on my own.
  • I would feel doomed if my dream job requires math.
  • I will not be able to choose a job which requires high mathematics skills.
  • I cannot compete with people who are naturally good at math and if I had to, I would be frustrated.

Other Findings:¶

  • Female students had higher MA than males.
  • Participants majoring in humanities and Social Sciences showed greater MA than those majoring in engineering and natural sciences.

Implications:¶

  • This research project provides the first validated scale of MA that can be administered to Korean college students.
